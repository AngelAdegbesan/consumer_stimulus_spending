How Are Americans Using Their Stimulus Checks?

March 23, 2021

By Angel Adegbesan

President Joe Biden signed the $1.9 trillion American Rescue Plan into law on March 12, allowing coronovarius relief to be extended to millions of Americans. The law is the third of its kind in a year since the coronavirus outbreak last March.

The First

As part of the lump-sum economic impact payments, qualifying adults have received $1,200, $600 and are bound to receive $1,400 in individual payments. According to a survey by the New York Federal Reserve done in Oct. 2020, about 18% of these funds was used for essentials (daily living expenses), 8% was used for non-essential (hobbies, leisure and vacation), 35% was used to pay down debt and about 36% of the payment was saved.

The Second

After the first round of payments, it became clear that people who are non-white, without a college degree and in lower-income households that have had financial setbacks because of the pandemic, are more likely to use a huge chunk their payments to pay down debts. The poll, done by SurveyMonkey, also showed that their counterparts, on the other hand, are more likely to save most of their payments. People of color are also more likely to spend a larger portion of their stimulus than white people.

Housing

26% of Black recipients said they used a large portion of their second stimulus payment on housing versus 12% of their white counterparts.

Savings

20% of White and 25% of Asian-Americans said they saved a portion or all of their second stimulus payment versus 9% of Black and 14% of Latinx Americans.

Survival

50% of Black people and 40% of Latinx people said they were counting on a third round of stimulus payment to get by, versus 22%, the share of Whites.

The Third

According to the Census Bureau's Household Pulse Survey done in Feb. 2021, household expenses and the reduction of debt continue to be the top priorities of majority of Americans who hope to receive their third stimulus check. The survey done in the week of Feb. 17 also shows that a majority plan to use the third round of payments on food and utilities.
